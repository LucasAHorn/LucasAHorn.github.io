About Me

Hello! I'm currently a junior at Iowa State University, pursuing a Bachelor's in Software Engineering. My interest in programming started when my dad introduced me to website development in 5th grade, and what began as curiosity quickly grew into a passion. After building a foundation in Python and Java during high school, I've since taken on more advanced coursework and hands-on projects at Iowa State, where I've had the chance to collaborate with talented peers and bring ideas to life.

Over the past few years, I've built Java projects applying algorithms and data structures, developed responsive web apps with React, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and designed relational and NoSQL databases with MySQL and MongoDB. I have also contributed to hackathon and team projects—like creating a animal prescription lookup tool for pharmacist Additionally, I enjoyed designing a study group app that pushed me to learn SpringBoot quickly and deal with issues related to scalability and usability. Through these projects, I've grown confident not just in my technical skills, but in my ability to solve problems creatively, adapt to new tools, and work well in a team.

Farm-aceutical Fix

I participated in the BuildDSM V1 hackathon with the theme of vet medicine, as part of a team of four. We developed a React application that provided easy navigation to information on animal prescriptions using the FDA Green Book. Our project's technical complexity and innovative approach earned us the 'Best Technical Build' award.
